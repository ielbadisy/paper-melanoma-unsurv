We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Survival Trajectory Phenotypes in Advanced Melanoma Under Systemic Therapy," for consideration as an Original Report in JCO Clinical Cancer Informatics.

In this study, we investigated whether individualized progression-free survival trajectories can be used to identify clinically interpretable prognostic phenotypes in advanced melanoma. We analyzed a single-center melanoma cohort treated with pembrolizumab or dacarbazine and applied a three-stage analytic workflow: deep survival modeling to estimate patient-level survival functions, unsupervised clustering of the predicted survival trajectories, and dynamic restricted mean survival time (RMST) to order and characterize the resulting phenotypes.

The manuscript may be of interest to the JCO Clinical Cancer Informatics readership because it applies biomedical informatics and survival-learning methods to a clinically relevant oncology problem: describing heterogeneous survival patterns under systemic therapy. Rather than focusing only on treatment-level contrasts or conventional prognostic-factor modeling, the analysis uses the predicted survival curve itself as the object of phenotype discovery, then summarizes the resulting groups on an interpretable time scale through dynamic RMST.

The study identified three ordered survival-trajectory phenotypes labeled low, medium, and high risk according to 24-month RMST. These phenotypes showed distinct predicted survival patterns, dynamic RMST trajectories, observed follow-up distributions, and Kaplan-Meier separation. We interpret the findings descriptively and emphasize that external validation in larger and more heterogeneous cohorts is required before clinical translation.
